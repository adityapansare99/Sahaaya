# Sahaaya — Food Safety Guidelines

## General food safety rules
All donated food must be safe for human consumption. Food should not be spoiled, contaminated, or past its safe consumption period. Donors are responsible for ensuring food quality at the time of pickup.

## Cooked meals
Cooked meals should be freshly prepared and stored at appropriate temperatures. Hot food should be kept above 60°C and cold food below 5°C. Cooked food should be consumed within 4 hours if kept at room temperature.

## Fresh vegetables and fruits
Wash all produce before donation. Remove any spoiled or damaged items. Store at appropriate temperatures. Leafy greens should be kept refrigerated.

## Packaged food
All packaged food must have intact seals and valid expiry dates. Do not donate damaged or bloated packages. Check for proper storage conditions.

## Dairy products
Dairy products must be kept refrigerated at all times. Check expiry dates. Do not donate products that have been left unrefrigerated for more than 2 hours.

## Bakery items
Bakery items should be fresh and free from mold. Store in clean, dry containers. Cream-filled items must be refrigerated.

## Beverages
All beverages must be in sealed containers. Check for leaks or damage. Carbonated drinks should not be shaken or stored in extreme temperatures.

## Food handling during transport
Delivery partners should transport food in clean vehicles. Keep different food types separated to prevent cross-contamination. Hot and cold items should be transported separately. Use insulated containers when possible.

## What to do if food is spoiled
If you receive spoiled food as an NGO, reject the delivery and report it through the platform. If you notice issues as a rider, contact the donor before pickup.
