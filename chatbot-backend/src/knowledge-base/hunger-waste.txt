# Sahaaya — Hunger & Food Waste in India

## Food waste statistics
Approximately 40% of food produced in India is wasted every year. This amounts to nearly 67 million tonnes of food waste annually. The financial value of this waste is estimated at around ₹92,000 crores.

## Hunger in India
Despite the food waste, millions of Indians go hungry every day. India ranks 107th out of 121 countries on the Global Hunger Index. An estimated 14% of India's population is undernourished.

## Environmental impact
Food waste contributes to greenhouse gas emissions. Decomposing food in landfills produces methane, a potent greenhouse gas. Reducing food waste is one of the most effective ways to combat climate change.

## How Sahaaya helps
Sahaaya connects surplus food from restaurants, events, and individuals to NGOs serving those in need. Every meal donated through the platform directly reduces food waste and fights hunger. The real-time tracking system ensures food reaches people while it's still fresh.

## The bigger picture
If 25% of the food currently wasted in India could be saved, it would feed the entire country's undernourished population. Platforms like Sahaaya are working to bridge this gap by making food donation easy, transparent, and efficient.
